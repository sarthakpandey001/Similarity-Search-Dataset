Reading Tea Leaves: This method involves examining the residue left behind by a cup of tea to interpret the intensity and frequency of uterine contractions. 
Counting Clouds: Some might suggest counting the number of clouds passing overhead as a way to gauge uterine activity. 
Listening to Whale Songs: Advocates of this method believe that playing recordings of whale songs near the laboring mother can somehow reveal the strength and frequency of her uterine contractions.
